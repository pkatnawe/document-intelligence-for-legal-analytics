Route the reader: OCR for clean, VLM for degraded revised by evidence
Originally "VLM over OCR, full stop." Then we benchmarked it (🔬 Experiments): on clean invoices, OCR-then-text is more accurate and cheaper (0.96 vs 0.88) — a small VLM misreads decimals and columns. So the decision changed to a cheap confidence-routed cascade: digital → text; scan → OCR when its confidence is high (clean), else the VLM (degraded, where OCR craters). A routing ablation beat both pure strategies at near-OCR cost, firing the VLM only 1/6 of the time. The VLM is the escalation reader, not the default; a type-specific prompt still guides it. ④
Optimize the classifier + prompts later
Ship hand-tuned today; once human-flagged examples accumulate, use GEPA ("optimize anything") to evolve the type-specific prompts and tune the classifier on the firm's real documents — sample-efficient, auditable. Evaluation

Still genuinely open

Deferred on purpose, to settle with real data rather than guess: which classifier the document mix needs (training-free feature-matching vs a small trained image model), and the exact confidence thresholds (tune once extractions accumulate — see Evaluation).

⚖️ Sensitive data & confidentiality — flagged, not building yet. Legal documents carry PII and privileged information, so a real engagement must decide what may be sent to which model at all — a question for the firm's experts, not us to assume. Open threads: which content is AI-eligible vs human-only; whether to redact PII before any model call; and model placement — our tier-1 gemini-3-5-flash is a global, cross-geo endpoint (data leaves the region) while tier-2 claude-opus-4-8 sits inside the Databricks perimeter, so sensitive documents would favour the in-perimeter model. We're not implementing this for the POC, but the design leaves room (Unity Catalog governance + a redaction step before extraction).
